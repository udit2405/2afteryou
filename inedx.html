How it works

A working through the AfterYou flow — from the moment you open the app to the moment your message reaches the person you sent it to.

1. You record

Open AfterYou in any modern browser. Choose video, audio, or a written letter. Your camera and microphone are accessed locally — nothing streams anywhere yet. Take as long as you need; re-record as many times as you want.

2. Your browser seals it

The moment you click Seal, your browser does three things, all locally on your own device:

  • Generates a fresh AES-256 encryption key — random bytes, never reused, never shared.
  • Encrypts your recording with that key (AES-GCM, the same standard used by Signal, WhatsApp, and your bank).
  • Uploads only the encrypted bytes to our storage. The key never touches our servers.

What we hold is gibberish. What you hold is the key.

3. You set the trigger

Pick when the message should arrive: a future date, immediately, kept private forever, or only if you stop responding to our heartbeat check-ins. Add the people who should receive it.

4. We deliver, you stay invisible

When the trigger fires, we email each recipient a private link. They open it; their browser fetches the encrypted bytes and decrypts them locally with the key embedded in the link. They watch the video, listen to the message, or read the letter. They can save it. They can keep it.

"At every step, the only person who can read your message is the person you sent it to."

Vault security

A plain-English explanation of how your messages are protected, what we can and cannot see, and what happens in the worst-case scenarios.

The encryption

We use AES-256-GCM, an authenticated encryption standard published by NIST and used worldwide for classified data. Every capsule gets its own fresh key. Keys are generated by your browser using the Web Crypto API, which has been audited by every major OS vendor.

What we can see

  • That you have an account.
  • The titles you give your capsules and which release mode you chose.
  • That a capsule exists and how many bytes of encrypted data it holds.
  • The email addresses of recipients you added (we need these to deliver).

What we cannot see

  • The contents of your video, audio, or text. Ever.
  • The encryption key that would let us decrypt it.
  • What recipients see when they open the message.

Worst-case scenarios — what's the damage?

Our database is breached

An attacker gets metadata: who has accounts, capsule titles, recipient emails. They get encrypted blobs that are mathematically useless without the key. No message contents are exposed.

Our storage is breached

An attacker gets ciphertext blobs only — no keys, no metadata, no way to know which blob belongs to which person.

Our company shuts down

See our Wind-down protocol — we publish a 90-day notice, give every user the chance to download their encrypted bundle and the keys they hold, and provide an open-source decryption tool so your messages survive us.

Independent review

Our cryptography stack is open-source and reviewable. See the Security audit page for the latest third-party report and bug bounty program.

Guardian network

The people who help us know when it's time. The Guardian network is the human safeguard behind the dead-man-switch — designed so a message never releases too early and never releases too late.

How it works

You name two to five Guardians — close friends, a sibling, a partner, a lawyer. They never see your messages or capsules. Their only job is to confirm one thing if asked: are you still with us?

The release sequence

  1. Heartbeat. We email you on the schedule you set (default: every 30 days). Tap "I'm here." Done.
  2. Soft escalation. Miss one heartbeat — we send three more, spaced over two weeks.
  3. Guardian alert. If you still haven't responded, we email your Guardians: "We've lost contact with [Name]. Are they okay?"
  4. Confirmation. Two Guardians must independently confirm before any release happens.
  5. Grace window. A final 7-day pause. We try to reach you one more time. If you log in during this window, everything resets.
  6. Release. Only then do scheduled "just-in-case" capsules go out.

What Guardians can and can't do

  • They can confirm or deny an escalation alert.
  • They can request a manual override if something has obviously happened (with documentation).
  • They cannot read any of your messages.
  • They cannot change your recipients, schedules, or settings.
  • They cannot trigger releases on their own — confirmation always requires two Guardians.

Our manifesto

Why we're building AfterYou, and why we promise to keep it the way it is.

Most of what we say to the people we love, we say in the wrong moment, or we don't say at all. We mean to. We rehearse it on long drives. And then we get distracted, or it's late, or we tell ourselves there will be more time.

Sometimes there isn't.

"AfterYou exists so that the things you wish you had said are actually said — even if you don't get to say them in person."

4. We move slowly on the things that matter.

We will not "move fast and break things" with messages people are trusting us to deliver decades from now. We will not pivot, ship features that compromise the security model, or sell to a company that would.

Wind-down protocol

What happens to your messages if AfterYou shuts down. We've planned for this so you don't have to worry about it.

The promise

If AfterYou ever ceases operations — for any reason: bankruptcy, acquisition we won't accept, founder death — we will give you at least 90 days of notice and an automated way to take everything with you.

The 90-day timeline

  1. Day 0 — Announcement. Email to every user. Banner on the website. Public post on our blog and socials. Press release if appropriate.
  2. Day 1 to 60 — Export window. Every user can download a single zip file containing all their encrypted capsules and metadata, plus the open-source decryption tool. Scheduled releases continue normally.
  3. Day 60 to 75 — Final reminders. Three more email reminders, plus an SMS to users who haven't exported yet.
  4. Day 75 to 90 — Manual rescue. Our team works one-on-one with anyone who hasn't been able to export.
  5. Day 90 — Wind-down. Service stops accepting new uploads. Existing data continues to be served read-only for an additional 30 days.
  6. Day 120 — Cryptographic destruction. All servers wiped. All keys destroyed. The decryption tool remains forever on GitHub for anyone holding their export bundle.

Who holds the keys?

An independent escrow agent holds a copy of our wind-down playbook, the open-source decryption tool's release artifacts, and contact information for our hosting providers. If our team is unable to execute the wind-down ourselves (the worst-case scenario), the escrow agent triggers it on our behalf.

Pre-paid users

If you paid annually and we wind down before your year is up, we refund the unused portion automatically.

Our hope

That this page never has to be used. But we'd rather plan for our own ending than ask you to trust us blindly.

One-time setup

Three steps. Takes ~10 minutes the first time.

1. Create a Supabase project

Go to supabase.com → New project. Pick the Mumbai region. Wait ~2 minutes for it to provision.

2. Run this SQL in the SQL Editor

Open SQL Editor in your Supabase dashboard, paste this, click Run.

-- ===== AfterYou — schema =====
create extension if not exists "uuid-ossp";

create table if not exists public.profiles (
  id uuid primary key references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  email text not null,
  display_name text,
  heartbeat_interval_days int not null default 30,
  last_check_in timestamptz not null default now(),
  missed_pings int not null default 0,
  status text not null default 'active',
  created_at timestamptz not null default now()
);

create table if not exists public.capsules (
  id uuid primary key default uuid_generate_v4(),
  owner_id uuid not null references public.profiles(id) on delete cascade,
  title text not null,
  format text not null check (format in ('video','audio','text')),
  storage_path text,
  text_ciphertext text,
  iv text not null,
  size_bytes bigint not null default 0,
  release_mode text not null check (release_mode in ('private','send_now','schedule','dead_man')),
  release_at timestamptz,
  released_at timestamptz,
  status text not null default 'sealed' check (status in ('sealed','released','revoked')),
  created_at timestamptz not null default now()
);
create index if not exists capsules_owner on public.capsules(owner_id);
create index if not exists capsules_due on public.capsules(release_mode, release_at) where status = 'sealed';

create table if not exists public.recipients (
  id uuid primary key default uuid_generate_v4(),
  capsule_id uuid not null references public.capsules(id) on delete cascade,
  email text not null,
  name text,
  notified_at timestamptz,
  created_at timestamptz not null default now()
);

create table if not exists public.trustees (
  id uuid primary key default uuid_generate_v4(),
  owner_id uuid not null references public.profiles(id) on delete cascade,
  email text not null,
  name text,
  relationship text,
  created_at timestamptz not null default now()
);

-- Auto-create profile on signup
create or replace function public.handle_new_user()
returns trigger language plpgsql security definer as $$
begin
  insert into public.profiles (id, email, display_name)
  values (new.id, new.email, coalesce(new.raw_user_meta_data->>'name', split_part(new.email, '@', 1)))
  on conflict (id) do nothing;
  return new;
end $$;
drop trigger if exists on_auth_user_created on auth.users;
create trigger on_auth_user_created after insert on auth.users
  for each row execute function public.handle_new_user();

-- Row Level Security
alter table public.profiles   enable row level security;
alter table public.capsules   enable row level security;
alter table public.recipients enable row level security;
alter table public.trustees   enable row level security;

drop policy if exists "p_self_read" on public.profiles;
create policy "p_self_read" on public.profiles for select using (auth.uid() = id);
drop policy if exists "p_self_write" on public.profiles;
create policy "p_self_write" on public.profiles for update using (auth.uid() = id);

drop policy if exists "c_owner_all" on public.capsules;
create policy "c_owner_all" on public.capsules for all
  using (auth.uid() = owner_id) with check (auth.uid() = owner_id);

drop policy if exists "r_via_capsule" on public.recipients;
create policy "r_via_capsule" on public.recipients for all using (
  exists (select 1 from public.capsules c where c.id = capsule_id and c.owner_id = auth.uid())
) with check (
  exists (select 1 from public.capsules c where c.id = capsule_id and c.owner_id = auth.uid())
);

drop policy if exists "t_owner_all" on public.trustees;
create policy "t_owner_all" on public.trustees for all
  using (auth.uid() = owner_id) with check (auth.uid() = owner_id);

-- Storage bucket for encrypted media
insert into storage.buckets (id, name, public)
values ('capsules', 'capsules', false)
on conflict (id) do nothing;

drop policy if exists "media_owner_rw" on storage.objects;
create policy "media_owner_rw" on storage.objects for all
  using (bucket_id = 'capsules' and (storage.foldername(name))[1] = auth.uid()::text)
  with check (bucket_id = 'capsules' and (storage.foldername(name))[1] = auth.uid()::text);

Optional: real email delivery (Edge Function)

Without this, capsules in "Send now" mode rely on you sharing the link manually from your vault. To auto-send via email:

  1. Sign up at resend.com — free 3,000 emails/month.
  2. In Supabase → Edge Functions → Create function send-capsule, paste the code below.
  3. Add secret: supabase secrets set RESEND_API_KEY=re_xxx
  4. For scheduled releases, enable the pg_cron + pg_net extensions in Supabase → Database → Extensions, and add the cron SQL below.
// supabase/functions/send-capsule/index.ts
// Deploy: supabase functions deploy send-capsule

import { serve } from "https://deno.land/std@0.224.0/http/server.ts";

serve(async (req) => {
  const { to, subject, html } = await req.json();
  const r = await fetch("https://api.resend.com/emails", {
    method: "POST",
    headers: {
      "Authorization": `Bearer ${Deno.env.get("RESEND_API_KEY")}`,
      "Content-Type": "application/json",
    },
    body: JSON.stringify({
      from: "AfterYou <hello@yourdomain.com>",  // ⚠️ change to your verified Resend domain
      to, subject, html,
    }),
  });
  return new Response(await r.text(), { status: r.status, headers: { "Content-Type": "application/json" }});
});

-- ===== AfterYou — scheduled release (run after Edge Function deployed) =====
create extension if not exists pg_cron;
create extension if not exists pg_net;

-- Hourly: deliver capsules whose scheduled time has arrived.
select cron.schedule('release-capsules', '0 * * * *', $$
  with due as (
    select c.id, c.title, c.owner_id, p.email as owner_email,
           p.display_name as owner_name
    from public.capsules c
    join public.profiles p on p.id = c.owner_id
    where c.status = 'sealed'
      and (
        (c.release_mode = 'schedule' and c.release_at <= now())
        or (c.release_mode = 'dead_man' and p.status = 'released')
      )
  )
  update public.capsules
     set status = 'released', released_at = now()
   where id in (select id from due)
   returning *;
$$);
