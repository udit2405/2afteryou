Quiet Questions

The things people
are afraid to ask.

What happens if AfterYou shuts down one day?

Your messages are encrypted with keys only you and your guardians hold. If we ever wind down, you receive a 12-month grace window to export everything as standard encrypted files — and a guardian protocol triggers regardless.

Can anyone — including AfterYou — read my messages?

No. We use zero-knowledge AES-256 encryption. We can confirm a message exists. We cannot open it. Not for police, not for family, not even by court order.

What if I want to delete a message I no longer believe?

You can edit, re-record, or destroy any message instantly while you're alive and verified. Past versions are wiped permanently. The only person who controls your archive is you.

How does the inactivity trigger know I'm really gone?

It's a layered check — push pings, email, SMS, then guardian verification. Most users set a 60-90 day window. We never deliver based on inactivity alone without human confirmation.

Is this morbid?

It's the opposite. Naming what you'd say if you couldn't say it again is a way of saying it now, louder, to yourself. Most users say recording made them call someone the same day.
